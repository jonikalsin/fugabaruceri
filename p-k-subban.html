P. K. Subban is a Canadian professional ice hockey player who has represented, as a defenceman, the Montreal Canadiens (2009-10 to 2015-16), the Nashville Predators (2016-17 to 2018-19), and the New Jersey Devils (2019-20-Present) in the National Hockey League (NHL). He was named in the ‘NHL All-Rookie Team’ (2011), the ‘NHL First All-Star Team’ (2013, 2015), and the ‘NHL Second All-Star Team’ (2018). For his performances, he had also been included in the ‘NHL All-Star Game’ for 3 years in a row (2016, 2017, 2018). Representing Canada, he had won the ‘Gold’ medal at the World Junior Ice Hockey Championships (2008, 2009), and the 2014 Winter Olympics. For his performances in the 2009 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, he was named in the tournament’s ‘All-Star Team’.

    P. K. Subban Facts

  • P. K. Subban had spent his junior career with the Belleville Bulls (2005-2006 to 2008-09) in the Ontario Hockey League (OHL). While playing for them, he had been drafted by the Montreal Canadiens in the off-season at the 2007 NHL Entry Draft.
  • In the 2007–08 season, he had helped the Belleville Bulls reach the J. Ross Robertson Cup Finals. He had taken the team to the semi-final the following season, his last with the team. He had been named in the ‘OHL First All-Star Team’ in 2009.
  • Before starting his NHL journey playing just 2 games for the Montreal Canadiens in 2009-10, he had spent the vast majority of the season representing the Hamilton Bulldogs in the American Hockey League (AHL), the primary developmental league for the NHL. For his performance, he had been named in the ‘AHL All-Rookie Team’, the ‘AHL All-Star Game, and the ‘AHL First All-Star Team’ in 2010.
  • For his performances in the 2013 NHL season, he had won the ‘James Norris Memorial Trophy’, awarded annually to the best defenceman in the league.
  • He was featured as the cover athlete for the video game NHL 19 developed by EA Sports.

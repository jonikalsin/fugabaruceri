Joel Madden

The lead singer of the American pop-punk band Good Charlotte is Joel Madden.

He is also well-known as a record producer, DJ, actor, and UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador. He is best known as one half of the popular pop-rock duo The Madden Brothers, along with his identical brother Benji Madden.

What is the Net Worth of Joel Madden? Salary, Earnings

As of October 2022, Joel Madden’s net worth is estimated to be $20 million. He makes a living as a record producer, singer, actor, and DJ, among other things. He also serves as a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador.

Where was Joel Madden born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Joel Madden was born in 1979 to Roger Combs and Robin Madden. He was born in the town of Waldorf, Maryland. Joel Madden has an identical twin named Benji Madden, who is a guitarist and backup vocalist in the band Good Charlotte.

He also has an older brother, Josh Madden, who works in the music industry, and a younger sister, Sarah Madden. Joel Madden was born on March 11, 1979, and will be 43 years old on July 30, 2022. He stands 1.7 m tall and weighs 75 kg.

How did Joel Madden start his Professional Career?

Joel Madden’s career began in 1995 when he was 16 when he formed a band with his brother Benji and a friend.The Maddens eventually hired associate high schoolers like Aaron Escolopio, Paul Thomas, and Billy Martin to help them establish their brand as Good Charlotte.

The name of this band was inspired by Carol Beach York’s children’s book Good Charlotte: Girls of the Good Day Orphanage. In the 2001 film Not Another Teen Movie, this vocalist and the other members of Good Charlotte played the band on the school walkway. Following that, he appeared in a cameo role in Fat Albert in 2004 and collaborated with his brother Benji in Material Girls in 2006.

Aside from his major works, he co-founded the clothing line MADE Clothing with two brothers, Benji and Josh. This label is no longer in business, and the clothing line was renamed DCMA Collective in early 2006. The singer rose to prominence in 2011 as one of four different vocal coaches on the previous season of an Australian version of the popular reality singing competition The Voice.
